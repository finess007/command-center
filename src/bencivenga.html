üîí The 11 Power Proof Elements

Session 4 ‚Äî The Secret of Master Closers

Anyone can make a promise, but nobody buys without belief. Proof is the rarest element in advertising ‚Äî and that's exactly why the brain trusts it most. The strongest ads pile layer upon layer of proof.

"Without belief, nobody buys. The higher the belief, the higher the response. Make compelling proof the alpha and omega of your advertising."
‚Äî Gary Bencivenga
Proof #1
Demonstration

No argument can compete with a strong demonstration. Show it working.

Otis demonstrating his elevator braking system in front of a crowd. "Do you make these mistakes in English?" with examples right in the copy.
Proof #2
Creative Guarantee

A bold, specific guarantee that becomes the selling point itself.

"Pizza in 30 minutes, guaranteed" ‚Äî built an empire for Domino's.
Proof #3
Reason / Justification

The word "because" adds instant believability. Give a reason ‚Äî even a simple one.

"50% sale" ‚Üí OK. "50% sale‚Ä¶ because of a recent fire" ‚Üí MUCH better. "Slice ‚Äî a better tasting juice‚Ä¶ because it has 10% real juice."
Proof #4
Specifics

Specific numbers and details signal research and truth. Vague = suspicious.

"Take only 50 days, spend just $11,076 for this 26,000-mile cruise to visit 21 countries." Beat the control by 250%: "62 of the most significant yet under-publicized medical discoveries of the past 3 years."
Proof #5
The Expos√©

Become the consumer's champion. Call out the bad actors in the market.

"The Great Vitamin Hoax" ‚Äî in a vitamin newsletter. "I recommend vitamins, but too many charlatans in the marketplace. Let me recommend the very best."
Proof #6
Explain the Mechanism

Show HOW it works, not just that it works. People trust what they understand.

"The Secret of Inevitable Wealth" ‚Äî then explains the full stock screening method step by step.
Proof #7
Sell Against Type

Make a case AGAINST something your market loves. Triggers massive curiosity.

"The case AGAINST tax exempts" beat "7 gold stocks that will soar" by multiple hundreds of percent. "Get rich slowly!" in a get-rich-quick market.
Proof #8
Acknowledge Disbelief

Name the skepticism before they feel it. Disarms resistance.

"Believe it or not, I've discovered‚Ä¶" / "Should any man be given so much sexual power over women?"
Proof #9
Highly Believable Source

Borrow credibility from a trusted authority.

"I'd advise you to subscribe" ‚Äî Warren Buffett. He tried to BUY the newsletter that got this quote ‚Äî they weren't milking it enough.
Proof #10
Testimonials

Must sound REAL, not polished. Imperfect language = believable.

"Guys like me need‚Ä¶" / "When I've cleared a million dollars I'll come buy you lunch." If you have none, have the owner give one with testimonial-style formatting.
Proof #11
Candor

Being less certain makes you MORE trusted. Admitting flaws disarms all objections.

"MAY rise" beat "WILL rise." "Fix ALMOST any part of ALMOST any car" beat the confident version. The North Pole ad listing every terrible thing about the expedition got massive response.

üéØ Trojan Horse Formats

The Welcome Package

"Here's your free report‚Ä¶ and Thank You!" ‚Äî a nicely designed book or ebook that looks like a welcome bonus for someone who already subscribed. People HAVE to know what's going on. They keep reading. Inside: an overview of everything you teach, with genuine value + a path to buy.

The Article Ad

In newspapers/magazines: make it look like editorial content. Use article formatting, not ad formatting. Same words, completely different response.

The Magalogue

Direct mail that looks like a magazine. Gets sorted with real magazines, sits on the coffee table for weeks. Sometimes the magalogue is only 40 pages shorter than the actual book.

The Value-First Copy

  • Guinness: Ad detailing how to pair oysters with beer ‚Äî valuable content
  • Detergent brand: Offering genuine stain removal advice
  • Ogilvy: Giving away 38 key advertising lessons in the ad itself
  • "Better than Botox?": The "?" makes it subtle ‚Äî raising a question, not touting

The Interview Model

"Breakthrough in malpractice protection ‚Äî an interview with Albert L. Koch." Interviews feel editorial, not commercial.

üìß Physical Trojan Horses

  • FedEx envelope: Nothing gets opened more ‚Äî use it if you really need to reach someone
  • Simple envelope: "Your little black book enclosed" / "Your Special Report enclosed" ‚Äî curiosity
  • The opt-in ratio: In DM, no more than half the space to sell. Online: 10-20% max selling, the rest = pure value

üî• The 10 Desires (The 10 R's)

Session 11 ‚Äî Mastering the Alchemy of Desire

These are the foundation of the Alessa copy framework. Every emotional angle maps to one of these 10 universal desires.

"Never try to manufacture desire. Much better to channel existing desires."
‚Äî Gary Bencivenga (also Eugene Schwartz, Breakthrough Advertising)
1. Recognition
Respect, fame, exclusivity, status, prestige, popularity. "Everyone asked where I got it."
2. Rejuvenation
Longevity, health, energy, fitness. Feeling younger, refreshed, alive.
3. Relaxation
Fun, leisure, having the luxury of time. Treating yourself.
4. Relief
From shame, pain, difficulty. "Finally something that fits." End the frustration.
5. Religion
Built-in beliefs. Use the patron saints of your market. Shared assumptions.
6. Remuneration
Money, wealth, financial security. Getting more value for your money.
7. Results
Tangible outcomes, measurable performance. "It actually works."
8. Revenge
"Show those who doubted you." Prove them wrong. Silent victory.
9. Role Performance
Be better in your role. Best mom, best professional, best version of you.
10. Romance
Travel, stimulation, excitement, seduction. The thrill of something new.

üìê Desire Rules

  • Infinite and never-ending ‚Äî desires are replaced almost as soon as fulfilled
  • Hard to create, easy to amplify ‚Äî don't sell aspirin to someone without a headache
  • Quantity ‚Üí Quality ‚Äî people start by accumulating, then want THE BEST
  • General ‚Üí Specialized ‚Äî desires subdivide. Sneakers ‚Üí running shoes ‚Üí trail runners. Always ask: where is the next subdivision?
  • Fear of loss >> desire for gain ‚Äî "Your snow tires are being stolen" gets you out of bed at 2 AM. "Free snow tires" doesn't.

üìù Street-Smart Copywriting Secrets

Sessions 6 & 9 ‚Äî 23 battle-tested principles

Headlines

  • Write 20-25 headlines minimum ‚Äî your 17th will be better than your 1st. Always.
  • Benefit headlines are 4x more likely to work than curiosity headlines
  • Best of all: combine benefit + curiosity ‚Äî "Secrets from the 'Black Book' of Office Politics"
  • Attack impossible objections head-on: "Buy shoes by mail? You must be kidding!"
  • "Breakthrough" is a word that always tests well
  • Multiple benefit headlines are extremely persuasive ‚Äî "Best performance, 20% projected profits, remarkably low risk"
  • Never write headlines before doing research ‚Äî or it will bias you
  • Collect great headlines daily ‚Äî Reader's Digest covers are gold

Body Copy

  • Body copy MUST continue from headline ‚Äî no dislocation, no speed bumps, smooth as silk
  • Never address readers as a group ‚Äî "Dear friends" / "folks" / "all of you" = NO
  • Bucket Brigade: "Moreover‚Ä¶" / "Not only that‚Ä¶" / "What's more‚Ä¶" / "And we're just getting started" ‚Äî pulls reader back if they try to start in the middle
  • Opening phrases: "Picture this‚Ä¶" / "Has this ever happened to you?" / "Now you can‚Ä¶" / "Imagine‚Ä¶"
  • "Hard to believe? Perhaps." ‚Äî disarms skepticism, switches off the "be careful" voice
  • Never leave a negative alone ‚Äî always follow with positive + proof
  • Use outlines for logical flow ‚Äî test: can you start reading mid-page and feel pulled back to the beginning?

Power Techniques

  • The "If‚Ä¶then‚Ä¶" technique: "If you've got 20 minutes a month, I guarantee a financial miracle." Make the "if" easy ‚Üí reader thinks "YES I can!"
  • Sell the premium, not the product: When in doubt, sell information. No commodity exists when combined with information.
  • The 30-Day Hold: "We won't charge for 30 days." Triples sales, doubles refunds ‚Äî but net contribution goes from $90 to $240.
  • Specialize the product: Vitamins for WOMEN. Vitamins for MEN OVER 50. They're 99.8% the same ‚Äî but people buy what addresses their segment.
  • "French women don't get fat" ‚Äî promise AND proof in the same short sentence.
  • "A special offer to readers of the NY Times" ‚Äî borrows the publication's endorsement without asking.
  • Aim for the white-hot center of your market. Go for the lingo. Don't dumb it down for the periphery.
  • When creativity fails, create an enemy.
  • If you are losing the battle, switch the battlefield. Don't sell books ‚Üí sell "the company of three wise men."

üé® Direct Response Design Rules

Session 10 ‚Äî Format often matters MORE than copy

Typography & Layout

  • Serif fonts > sans-serif ‚Äî 86% difference in testing. People tire faster reading sans-serif.
  • Visual on top, headline underneath ‚Äî don't fight "reading gravity"
  • Left to right, top to bottom ‚Äî visual goes left or top (attracts eyes first)
  • Column width: Max 40-50 characters (like newspapers)
  • Photos > illustrations ‚Äî proven over and over. Photos have real credibility.
  • Start body copy with drop caps (big first letter, like magazines)
  • Avoid reverse type (white on black) ‚Äî kills readability

Punctuation & Flow

  • üö´ Never put a period after a headline ‚Äî a period means STOP. It stops the reader.
  • üö´ Never end a page with a period ‚Äî use cliffhangers to pull to next page
  • ‚úÖ Photo captions are gold ‚Äî one of the most-read elements. Don't waste that spot.
  • ‚úÖ PS = most widely read thing in a letter after the headline
  • ‚úÖ Keep paragraphs short (5-6 lines max)
  • ‚úÖ Sub-heads = recharging stations ‚Äî write them like mini headlines
  • ‚úÖ Don't kill widows (2-3 words on last line) ‚Äî they make copy easier to read

Format Rules

  • Long copy outpulls short copy ‚Äî if it's readable
  • Letter works without brochure ‚Äî brochure without letter never works
  • Letter = warm, personal. Brochure = flashy but looks like an ad. Sometimes brochure gets thrown away before they read the letter.
  • Always view your ad the way prospects will ‚Äî don't approve on a giant screen what'll be seen on a phone
  • Contact info on EVERY piece ‚Äî people keep one piece and order later
  • If coupon in ad: never let a photo interrupt the coupon outline ‚Äî people resist cutting photos, especially of humans
